Business Etiquette

    As you have likely been told time and time again, it takes only ten or 15 seconds to make a first impression and the rest of your life to undo it.With practice you can learn topresent yourself as a polished yourself as a polished professional every time you meet someone.Here are some guidelines to get you off on the right foot.

    Your five business introduction rules
      1. Stand up: When you are seated and someone apporaches you to say hello with a handshake, standup,Whether you are a man or woman.By doing so you show the person tou are greeting them that they are worth your full attetion.
      2.Make Eye Contact:By looking someone directl,in the eye you accomplished three things:
      -you will project an image of self-confidence and a healthy self-esteem
      -You will be perceived as a good listener
      -You will probably receive the same courtesy when you speak.
      Creating a BAD First Impression
      Sloppy Language: No matter how well you dress, using sloppy language will lessen your credibility with others. For example saying "yeah" instead of "yes", or using profanity, especially around people that would not find it impressive. Most people consider your choice of words a direct reflection on your intelligence.
      Giggling: While laughter can be the best medicine for many ills, giggling can be annoying
      Hiding your Hands: Believe it or not, etiquette research has shown that hands above board are more appealing than hands hidden away in pockets. When your hands are not showing, it often sends a signal to someone that you are not telling all or that you are holding something back
      o Gum-Chewing: Let there be no doubt about it, you should not chew gum when meeting people. No exceptions.
      o Throat Clearing: The habit of clearing your throat several times during a conversation can be very distracting.
